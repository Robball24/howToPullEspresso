A Quick Guide to Pulling Espresso in a Gaggia Classic Pro

Here is what you'll need.

  • Gaggia Classic Pro
  • Coffee Beans
  • Coffee Grinder
  • Tamper
  • 2 oz measuring glass
  • Timer

Okay, let's get started!

  1. Turn the Gaggia Classic Pro on and let it warm up for 20-30 minutes.
  2. Grind coffee beans to a fine grind, a little finer than sugar.
  3. Fill portafilter with coffee grinds, and tamp.
  4. Grab a frothing pitcher, or some container that can hold hot water.
  5. Place the container under the shower screen, and turn the brew switch until the light turns off, then turn the brew switch off.
  6. Wipe the shower screen dry, and wait for the light to turn back on.
  7. Once the light is on, turn the steam switch on for 5 seconds.
  8. Place the portafilter in its place, and place the container under the steam wand.
  9. PLace the 2 oz measuring glass under the portafilter.
  10. Turn on the steam wand, and flip the brew switch immediately after.
  11. After 7 seconds, turn the steam wand off.
  12. As soon as coffe starts to pour, start the timer.
  13. The goal is to get 2 ozs(counting the crema) in 20-30 secons.

Some Trouble Shooting!

  • If your shot doesn't pull, you tamped too hard.
  • If it pulls too quickly, then you either tamped it too soft, or more likely, didn't grind the coffee fine enough.
